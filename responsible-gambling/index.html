Recognizing a Gambling Problem

It is important to regularly assess your betting habits. You may be developing a gambling problem if you experience any of the following:

  • Chasing losses by betting more money than you can afford to lose.
  • Lying to family and friends about the amount of time or money spent gambling.
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund your betting account.
  • Feeling anxious, depressed, or stressed due to your gambling activities.
  • Neglecting work, school, or personal relationships in favor of betting.
